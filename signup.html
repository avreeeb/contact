PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS

TheGFS Program is an intensive and challenging program requiring an exceptionally high level of commitment from all participants, particularly during the Program's first course, GhettoFilm101 (see Fellows Program summary document).

During this time, officially scheduled classes meet Monday through Thursday, 10am-5pm. In addition, students and instructors often times spend weekends and evenings at GFS when additional time is necessary. Do not apply to this program if you are bound to any prior summer engagements. Perfect attendance is mandatory. The value of your experience depends on maintaining great dedication to our program and your fellow GFS filmmakers.

Questions? Call 347-306-4332 or send an email to ajohnson@ghettofilm.org

QUESTION 1

Describe a person, fictional or real, that you believe would be a fascinating character to watch in a film. Describe what makes this individual particularly interesting. What does he/she look like (clothing, hair, etc.)? What does this person sound like? What is the world this person moves in? Why are they a good character for a movie?

QUESTION 2

If you were going to make a movie that took place entirely in one building (e.g. a store, a restaurant, a school, an apartment complex, a hospital), what building would you choose and why? Who would be the main characters in your story? What would be some of the visually interesting details from inside this building that you would want to show your audience?

QUESTION 3

Describe the opening scene for a movie that you would like to produce. Choose a situation you feel would grab an audience's attention. Where is the scene located? Who is in the scene? Is there any dialogue? Is there any music? Be as descriptive as possible.
